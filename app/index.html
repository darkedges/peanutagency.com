BACK WHEN WE WERE JUST NUTS

 
 

Whilst Peanut Agency is brand new, this isn't the first time some of us have worked together. Here’s some of the missions we’ve recently returned from:

 
 
 

  • THE DIM SIM SALUTE


    Melbourne is a place boasting many wonders, yet there was one thing the city longed for: a bronze statue commemorating the dim sim. Considered Western by the East, and Eastern by the West, this Melbourne invention was perfect symbol to celebrate our multicultural society. The Dim Sim Salute was a campaign that set out to give the humble dim sim the recognition it deserved by coating it in bronze for future generations to marvel upon.

    The grassroots movement began on social media and soon gained national coverage as the Lord Mayor Robert Doyle (a devoted meat pie man) expressed his opposition to the idea.

     

  • ASOS Sync & Style

    An individual’s style is greatly influenced by their music idols, music genres and music culture. Sync & Style is an application which syncs with the users music and suggests personalised ASOS styles to match. By showcasing the link between fashion and music, this app will connect users in a social network of style, personality, creativity and inspiration.

  • Dirty Hands

    Dirty Hands is a stop motion animation that won Scott a seat at the prestigious D&AD New Blood programme in partnership with The Glue Society and Google. The workshop provides mentorship from some of the industry’s finests and celebrates excellence and outstanding talent in the creative field. We are thrilled that Scott was able gain a place in this valuable programme and be recognised for his exceptional work.

     

  • Stavangerfjord

    The Stavangerfjord cruiseferry is the first in the world to be powered exclusively by liquefied natural gas. Connecting Norway and Denmark in just a few hours, the new ferry meant that families do not have to travel far to have a great time away. This ambient campaign showed just how simple it is to travel on the Stavangerfjord - it’s just a matter of driving on board!

  • Kathmandu Trek Tips

    Kathmandu Trek Tips is your own tour guide in your pocket. It engages with Kathmandu customers in a way that inspires them and rewards them for their active lifestyle. By using NFC (near field communication) totems, users can install the app along their journey - even if they don’t have a phone signal. Hikers can uncover all sorts of information using Trek Tips, plus they can leave messages, pointers and ratings for other Trek Tips travellers to discover making their journey customisable and sharable.

    This project is a conceptual mock-up made in conjunction with past RMIT students.

     

  • PlayDate App

    The dating world can be daunting. One wrong move and you can say goodbye to scoring a second date faster than you can say, “so… are you up for a threesome?”

    PlayDate helps take the awkwardness and uncertainty out of dating by analysing data based on the user’s mood, budget, location and time of day. Full of spontaneous ideas and loaded with a supreme sense of humour, PlayDate helps take the pressure off dating, gets people laughing, and gives couples loads of ways to make date night a great night.

  • Coles Secret Specials

    New technology could help upgrade the current Coles application to give it a serious competitive edge. Utilising technology like iBeacons will allow Coles to give their shoppers an incentive to use the app by creating something unique, fun and interactive which not only helps them to be organised, but helps them save money.

  • Dr. Martens Solemates

    A significant number of Dr. Marten wearers are supporters of, or part of, the LBGT community. The idea behind Dr. Marten’s Solemates, is to increase sales and dominance in the Australian market by making a bold statement about their support for the LBGT community. With a mix of both traditional forms of media and digital technology, the campaign aims to create discussion and express the brand’s beliefs which ties in closely with their brand image.
